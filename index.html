I am Haichang(Charles) Li(李海畅), currently a second-year Undergraduate student at Purdue University, majoring in Computer and IT, and minoring in Communication. Before coming to Purdue University, I dropped out of the EEE joint program of XJTLU and UoL for personal planning, so I also have learned some hardware knowledge in the Year-1 @XJTLU.

My research interests are at the intersection of HCI, music processing, NLP, social science, and embodied AI, and I hope to explore how to design trustworthy AI and how to make AI more human-like in its perception of the world, and Human-AI Collaboration in this process. For now, my interest is in AI-assisted technology, and I hope to use AI to help people who are psychologically or physically disadvantaged to make the world fair.
More broadly, I hope to explore machine-perceived interactions (multimodal learning) and their impact on people and society (HCI&AI4Social Science).

More Details Of Me

My ultimate goal is to enable machines to perceive the world like humans and coexist peacefully with humanity in the future. With the development of multimodal deep learning and large models, I believe machines will first achieve embodied intelligence, with genuine sensory perception and interaction between perceptions. They will also approximate having a "self" using the AGI "1-to-many" capabilities of large models. However, my ideal vision for machines is akin to the "550W-MOSS" digital lifeform from "The Wandering Earth," where they may possess their own thoughts, though I'm uncertain.

Multimodal deep learning

- Can we enable machines to cross-connect various modalities to enhance their comprehensive and realistic perception of the external world, particularly through aligning and generating various modalities?

LLM&NLP with AGI

- Facing LLMs, I'm amazed by their ability to interact with humans using language, resembling thought. Humans rely on brainwaves to receive and unify multimodal information. Can LLMs serve as the "brain" of machines, using text as the medium to unify various modalities in an “External-Prompt-LLM-Code-Agent” pattern? Is this approach feasible and stable?

HCI&AI4 Social Science

- What are the impacts of LLMs on humans and society? Can we improve their interpretability and trustworthiness by studying their effects on humans and society? For instance, there's a theory in communication called "embodied communication." Can this perspective guide the communication between Machines and Humans&World?

  • The Post-COVID-19 era has brought a cold winter to the global job market, with companies announcing layoffs and hiring freezes. At the time we created Shine Resume, youth unemployment was over 20 percent, according to the Chinese Bureau of Statistics, and the percentage of recent graduates who were successfully employed, according to our sample, was only 30 percent. In particular, those who are already disadvantaged in the job market - those mid-level graduates from non-" 985&211 "universities, most of them have no academic aura, and the three years spent in Covid-19 university have left most of them without any outstanding experience and wondering whether they have accomplished nothing. So we thought, what can we do for them? So we integrated LLM and AIGC technology and deployed a variety of tests and psychological guidance to help them break through the information gap and find their own shine. We plan to deploy a whole industry chain, including designing various resume formats that have never been seen in the market, such as foldable or poster, writing resumes through LLM, generating identification photos for them through AIGC, and integrating resources to help them learn job-hunting skills...... We want all ordinary people to be able to find something wonderful about themselves and shine.

  • Music to AI
  • Have you ever thought about synesthesia? There are about 200 million hearing impaired people in the world, and the music we normally enjoy is a luxury for them. For example, when we hear grand piano music, we may conjure up images of medieval battlefields, but those who are equally imaginative and hard of hearing cannot. So, our project is willing to be their ears, based on emotions and AIGC and LLM, we let them use the "see" way to "hear" the music. In the face of the injustices they suffer, our answer takes its own approach, using technology to provide them with a potentially viable approach.
