My AI Value

My ultimate goal is to enable machines to perceive the world like humans and coexist peacefully with humanity in the future. With the development of multimodal deep learning and large models, I believe machines will first achieve embodied intelligence, with genuine sensory perception and interaction between perceptions. They will also approximate having a "self" using the AGI "1-to-many" capabilities of large models. However, my ideal vision for machines is akin to the "550W-MOSS" digital lifeform from "The Wandering Earth," where they may possess their own thoughts, though I'm uncertain.

Now, I'm curious about three questions:

  1. Can we enable machines to cross-connect various modalities to enhance their comprehensive and realistic perception of the external world, particularly through multimodal deep learning, including aligning and generating various modalities?

  2. Facing LLMs, I'm amazed by their ability to interact with humans using language, resembling thought. Humans rely on brainwaves to receive and unify multimodal information. Can LLMs serve as the "brain" of machines, using text as the medium to unify various modalities in an ‚ÄúExternal-Prompt-LLM-Code-Agent‚Äù pattern? Is this approach feasible and stable?

  3. What are the impacts of LLMs on humans and society? Can we improve their interpretability and trustworthiness by studying their effects on humans and society? For instance, there's a theory in communication called "embodied communication." Can this perspective guide the communication between Machines and Humans&World?
